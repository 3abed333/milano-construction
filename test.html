Petra, in Jordan, known as the "Rose City," is a fascinating archaeological site carved into vibrant pink cliffs around 6th century BCE. It features iconic structures like the Treasury and the Monastery, showcasing Nabataean rock-cut architecture and clever water systems. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Petra reflects its past as a bustling trading hub, drawing visitors with its enduring beauty and cultural importance.
